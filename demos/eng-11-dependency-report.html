Recommended upgrade order · 6 steps this quarter

  1. corelib-httpclient 4.2.9 → 5.1.0 L Two open advisories and the widest blast radius (14 modules); three other libraries pin its 4.x API transitively, so everything downstream waits on this.
  2. retryflow 0.7.2 → 0.8.0 S 0.8 is the first release built against corelib-httpclient 5.x: a one-line bump once step 1 lands.
  3. authtoken-kit 2.1.3 → 2.3.0 M 2.3 drops the 4.x client shim; upgrading before step 1 would force a temporary compatibility layer we would throw away.
  4. fastjson-parser 2.8.1 → 3.0.4 M Closes ADV-2026-0061 (fictional advisory); 3.x renames the streaming API, touching 11 call sites, mechanical but must be reviewed.
  5. yaml-config-lite 1.4.0 → 1.6.2 S Closes ADV-2026-0049 (fictional advisory); config loading is behind one wrapper class, so the surface is small.
  6. pdfforge-lite 0.9.4 → 1.2.0 or replace M The 1.x licence change is flagged strong copyleft: hold the upgrade until legal review concludes; evaluate a permissive alternative in parallel.

Why corelib-httpclient must go first

Three dependencies in this service compile against the corelib-httpclient 4.x API. Until it moves to 5.x, their own latest versions cannot be adopted without throwaway shims.
